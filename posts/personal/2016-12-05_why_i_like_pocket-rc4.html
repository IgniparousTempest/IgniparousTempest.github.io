Why I like the Pocket-RC4 algorithm.

See here for a technical description.

In early 2015 myself and a friend participated in an annual info sec competition called HackFu, hosted by MWR labs. There were 9 challenges in total, and the first 10 people to finish them would win a trip to England to participate in a conference/competition. The first of these challenges consisted of a veiled reference to a Pocket-RC4 cipher. This was the last challenge we finished and the challenge we struggled with the most, not because it was the most mentally taxing, but because the passcode we received was very different to the format of the other challenges. What we thought was gibberish tuned out to be Latin. The phrase we decoded was "corvusoculumcorvinoneruit", after building many permutations of this algorithm, my partner noticed the spacing of consonants and vowels and figured that this might be Latin. It tuned out to be corvus oculum corvi non eruit, meaning "a crow will not pull out the eye of another crow.".

We initially assumed it was the Solitaire cipher we were dealing with. After a while we discovered the Pocket-RC4 cipher, with only the original paper and one blog post describing it. During the hours we were stuck on this problem a deck of cards were always present in my hands.

And we won!

We submitted our solutions to all nine challenges and we won a trip to England. It was my first time visiting another continent. I grew up in very rural parts of Southern Africa, in the Namib desert of southern Namibia and the rolling hills of the Eastern Cape. Visiting a first world country was quite a culture shock. Conversing with Europeans was quite interesting, in the sense that: the things that we took for granted about our own worlds fascinated the other. I was in awe of trains that moved beneath the city, and they were equally in awe of tales of wildlife that freely wondered through my home town. I saw a gemsbok breaking into our yard to steal our figs as a nuisance, they saw it as a tale of wonderment. They saw the daily underground commute as a waste of time, I saw it as an adventure.

Contrary to expectations

Being an English speaker that grew up in the south of Namibia, it was quite normal that many people could not understand my language. In level of prevalence the languages spoken in this region were: Afrikaans > Oshiwambo > German > English. I expected London to be something out of a Harry Potter movie. Contrary to my expectations, there were not many English present in London and far less English speakers than I had expected. London had contained many surprises, including a group of girls who invited us to go clubbing with them, only to witness these girls initiate a fist fight with a group of men; something I have yet to bear witness to again.

All thanks to Pocket-RC4

Thanks to Pocket-RC4 I got to experience a new continent for the first time, and for this experience this algorithm will always have a special place in my thoughts.
